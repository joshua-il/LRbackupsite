  It will require only fifty thousand U.S. dollars above routine operating expenses to build the first phase of the planned facility. In addition to satisfying the requirement to improve the property the new facility will better enable Little Roses staff to produce self reliant and self-supporting citizens.

To achieve this goal, Little Roses will simultaneously start workshops for garment making, develop dairy and poultry farms. They will train students for electrical, electronic, carpentry, masonry occupations. There is a shortage of skilled labor and technicians in this part of India.

Women, who possess an educational background, receive assistance from local institutions based on their background. Some women are helped to get admission/employment in nursing homes, girl's school hostel etc.
  • There is no age limit for girls and grown up women.
  • Caste, religion or community is not a bar for admission here.
  • They should be orphans.
  • Children from dysfunctional families.
  • Children of chronically ill parents- mother/father (T.B., Asthma, Cancer, AIDS etc).
  • Children of poor parents living in far off places without transporting facilities like hilly areas and remote villages.
  • In cities like Bombay, normally the children of Tamil parents live in socially affected environment. There is a chance for these children to turn out to be anti-social elements. Such children, recommended by local Social Welfare Organizations, are admitted at this Trust.
  • Widows/destitute women.
  • Though there is no age limit to girls, only boys under the age of 10 are admitted.
  • The children of those parents going to other states for seasonal work.
  • Social Welfare Hostels are run by the government for socially handicapped children. But these children can study only up to 5th grade. Then they are forced to discontinue their education. Such children may stay at the Trust and continue their studies.

After completing the construction of a new hostel building, 'Little Roses Trust' will be shifted to the new building at Rajapalayam, about 150km away from Dindugal, its present location. The same branch will be maintained at Dindugal too. This building needs to be completed to facilitate functioning of the Children's Home from the academic year of June, 2003.

5 staffs will be appointed for the present Children's Home with a remuneration of about US $100.00 each.

Hereafter the new Head Office of this Trust will function from the new office building at Rajapalayam immediately after the completion of the same.
